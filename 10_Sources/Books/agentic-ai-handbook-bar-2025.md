# The Agentic AI Handbook: Concepts, Design Patterns, and Future Directions

> Full citation: Kaushik Bar. "The Agentic AI Handbook: Concepts, Design Patterns, and Future Directions." 2025.

## Summary

The book surveys agentic AI across five parts, moving from philosophical foundations to future research directions. Part I defines agentic AI against reactive and narrow AI, and grounds the definition in philosophy, cognitive science, and robotics, covering goals, planning, self-reflection, situatedness, embodiment, and autonomy. Part II unpacks the technical building blocks: planning and goal-directed behaviour, reinforcement and meta-learning, memory and world modelling, symbolic and statistical reasoning, and uncertainty and meta-cognition. Part III surveys architectures, including Belief-Desire-Intention models, cognitive architectures such as SOAR and ACT-R, hybrid symbolic and sub-symbolic systems, large language model-based agent frameworks, open-ended learning agents, multi-agent ecosystems, and embodied agents. Part IV covers applications in robotics, autonomous vehicles, education, and scientific discovery, alongside societal, economic, and governance impact. Part V looks toward pathways to artificial general intelligence, design principles for safe and interpretable agents, and open frontiers in emotion-aware and self-repairing systems. The author positions the book as a reference for researchers and practitioners, and a guide for policymakers, educators, and industry leaders.

## Key Concepts

- Agentic AI sets goals, reasons about the world, adapts strategies, and acts autonomously in dynamic environments, in contrast to reactive systems that only respond to inputs.
- Situatedness, embodiment, and autonomy distinguish agentic systems from conventional AI.
- Belief-Desire-Intention models and cognitive architectures such as SOAR and ACT-R offer symbolic blueprints for agency.
- Large language model-based agent frameworks form a distinct architectural paradigm alongside symbolic and hybrid systems.
- Multi-agent ecosystems and open-ended learning agents extend single-agent design toward collective and continual adaptation.

## Terminology

- **Agentic AI** — a system that sets goals, plans, self-reflects, and acts autonomously, as opposed to a purely reactive system.
- **Situatedness** — the property of an agent being embedded in, and responsive to, a specific environment.
- **Belief-Desire-Intention (BDI)** — an architecture that models an agent's state as beliefs, desires, and intentions.
- **Cognitive architecture** — a structured framework, such as SOAR or ACT-R, for modelling general intelligent behaviour.
- **Open-ended learning** — an agent's capacity to keep acquiring new skills and knowledge without a fixed task boundary.

## Best Practices

The foreword frames agentic AI design as requiring a balance of autonomy with human-aligned values, and pairing every application with robust safeguards. The stated key takeaways stress that architectural choices shape both capability and risk, and that reliability depends on alignment and interpretability.

## Warnings and Anti-Patterns

The author warns against treating agentic AI as a simple extension of reactive, narrow AI. The book frames unchecked autonomy as a source of ethical dilemmas, governance gaps, and safety concerns that demand careful design and oversight from the outset.

## Future Work

Part V sets out the book's forward-looking agenda: pathways to artificial general intelligence, design principles for safe and interpretable agents, and open challenges in emotion-aware systems, self-repairing systems, and human-AI hybrid cognition. The closing chapter argues that the trajectory of agentic AI depends on deliberate human choices about design, governance, and integration, not on technological capability alone.
